What is Poverty?

The most widely held and understood definition of absolute poverty measures poverty strictly in economic terms — earning less than $1.90 a day. By this definition, poverty is the condition where a person lacking financial resources is unable to meet a minimum standard of living.

This poverty definition encompasses living conditions, an inability to meet basic needs because food, clean drinking water, proper sanitation, education, health care and other social services are inaccessible.

NEARLY 10 PERCENT of the world's population live in extreme poverty, below the $1.90 per day poverty line.

Source: World Bank

Poverty During COVID-19

The unemployment rate during this pandemic is very high and millions report that their households did not get enough to eat or are not caught up on rent payments.

The impacts of the pandemic and the economic fallout have been widespread, but are particularly prevalent among Black, Latino, Native American, and immigrant households. These disproportionate impacts reflect harsh, longstanding inequities — often stemming from structural racism — in education, employment, housing, and health care that the current crisis is exacerbating.

Causes For Global Poverty



Inequality and Marginalization

For a population to escape poverty, all groups must be involved in the decision-making process.

Conflict

Conflict is one of the most common forms of risk driving poverty today. One of the main conflicts being violence

Hunger/Malnutrition

You might think that poverty causes hunger (and you would be right!), but hunger is also a cause, and maintainer, of poverty.

Healthcare

Extreme poverty and poor health often go hand in hand. Easily preventable and treatable illnesses can be fatal.

Climate Change

Climate change creates hunger, whether through too little water or too much, and its effects contribute to poverty

Public Works

A lack of public infrastructure can prevent people from being able to work or get an education contributing to poverty

Government Support

Not every government can provide this type of help to its citizens, letting them go further into extreme poverty
